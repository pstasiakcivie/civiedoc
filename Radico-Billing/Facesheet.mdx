# **Navigate to Facesheet**

<Steps>
  <Step title="To access the Facesheet interface:" icon="chevron-down">
    1. Navigate to the **Facesheet** tab from the left navigation menu.
    2. Use the **Search** bar to locate documents by **file name**.
    3. Click **Filter** to refine the results using one or more criteria:
       - Created Date Range
       - Client Name
       - File Name
       - Document Status
    4. Click **Export** to download the filtered list in \*\*CSV \*\*or \*\*Exormat.cel \*\*f
    5. Click the **View** button (in the \*\*Actions \*\*column) to open a specific facesheet document.
  </Step>
</Steps>

# **View Facesheet**

This section guides users through viewing, editing, and managing facesheet records in the RCM application.

![F6 Pn](/images/f6.png)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Facesheet">
    **To open a facesheet**: Click **View** from the **Actions menu (⋮)**  next to the desired record.

    ![F5 Pn](/images/f5.png)

    A split-screen interface appears:

    **Left Panel**: Displays the uploaded facesheet in PDF format.

    **Right Panel:** Displays all patient forms associated with the facesheet, such as **Patient Demographics, Insurance, Address**, and more. Enter the corresponding details as provided in the facesheet.

    <Note>
      **Document Viewer Note**:\
      If the PDF fails to load, a message appears: "_An error occurred while loading the document. Try reloading the page_."
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Document Status">
    The current document status appears at the top of the right panel and updates automatically as form progresses, the status can be as follow: 

    | Status                            | Description                             |
    | :-------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------- |
    | **<Tooltip tip="">New</Tooltip>** | Document has not been opened or edited. |
    | **In Progress**                   | Partially completed.                    |
    | **Completed**                     | All required fields are filled.         |
    | **Invalid**                       | Document has been marked as incorrect.  |

    To manually update the status- Click the **Actions** button at the top right. Choose **Mark as Complete** or **Mark as Invalid** based on the document's state.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mark as Invalid" stepNumber={3}>
    The **Mark as Invalid** feature allows users to flag a facesheet document as invalid if it cannot be used or processed. Here’s how you can complete this action:

    ![F22 Pn](/images/f22.png)

    1. **Select a Reason**\
       Use the **Reason** dropdown to choose the appropriate reason for marking the document invalid (e.g., _Can't Open_).
    2. **Add Comment (Optional)**\
       Enter any additional information or context in the **Comment** box to help clarify the issue.
    3. **Submit or Cancel**

       Click **Mark as Invalid** to confirm and save the changes and click **Cancel** if you do not wish to proceed.

    <Note>
      Once marked invalid, the document will be moved to Invalid status.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Comments (If any)">
    Click **Comments** to view any existing remarks for the facesheet.

    <img
      src="/images/f7.png"
      alt="2025 07 23 17 38 40 Pn"
      title="2025 07 23 17 38 40 Pn"
      className="mx-auto"
      style={{ width:"82%" }}
    />

    Use the **Text Box and Attachment**  option to add new comments or upload related files.

    If there are no comments, the message **No comments found** will be displayed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## **Add New Record**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review Patient Information">
    Review the facesheet PDF on the left panel and extract the required patient details for entering into the form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Identify if the record belongs to an existing patient:">
    Click **Search** **Patient** button at the top of the right panel.

    ![F9 Pn](/images/f9.png)

    Apply filters to search the required patient. If found, select the patient to auto-fill the form.

    ![F9 1 Pn](/images/f9.1.png)

    If **patient not found** proceed to create a new record using the form provided below.
  </Step>
  <Step title="For a New Patient">
    Manually enter the following patient details from the facesheet. Complete all required fields (marked with **\***): **Name**, **Date** **of** **Birth**, **Gender**, **SSN**, **Contact**, **Address**, **Employer** **Encounter** **Number** and **MRN**.

    ![F10 Pn](/images/f10.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Guarantor Details">
    If guarantor is**same as patient** , select the checkbox to auto-fill.

    If different enter **Name, DOB, Address, Phone, SSN, Gender, Employer,** and **Relationship**.

    ![F11 Pn](/images/f11.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Admission Information">
    Enter **Patient Class, Location, Place of Service, Admit Date,** **Discharge Dates**

    and **Referring Physician's Name**.

    ![F12 Pn](/images/f12.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Primary Insurance Information">
    Fill the details **Plan ID, Insurance Provider, Group Numbers, Insured Person’s Name, DOB, Address, SSN, Gender, Employer,** and **Relationship.**

    ![F13 Pn](/images/f13.png)

    <Note>
      **Note**

      Expand each section and fill all required fields. You may also add **Secondary or Tertiary** insurance if applicable.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save Record">
    Click **Save** to store the entered data. The facesheet status changes to **In Progress**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="If there are multiple records in the same facesheet" stepNumber={8}>
    After saving the first record, click the **Add New Record** button located at the top of the right panel.

    ![1 Pn](/images/f8.png)

    Enter the next patient’s details following the same steps as above. Repeat this process for each additional patient listed in the facesheet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mark as Complete" stepNumber={9}>
    Once data entry is finished, select **Mark as Complete** from the **Actions** menu to change the status to **Completed**.

    ![1 Pn](/images/f14.png)
  </Step>
</Steps>

### **Link Patient to Facesheet**

This function ensures the correct patient is connected to the facesheet.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate the unlinked facesheet record.">
    Click the **Link** icon.

    ![F16 Pn](/images/f16.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search and Select Patient">
    Use filters like **Name**, **Last Name**, **MRN**, or **SSN**. Click the arrow icon  <Icon icon="arrow-right-long" /> to select.

    ![F16 Pn](/f17.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and Update Details">
    The form auto-fills with patient data. Validate fields such as:

    - Name, DOB, Gender, SSN
    - Phone, Address, State, ZIP Code
    - Complete any required fields and click **Save** to link the patient.

    <img
      src="/images/f18.png"
      alt="F18 Pn"
      className="mx-auto"
      style={{ width:"85%" }}
    />
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Page Range After Saving">
    After clicking the **Save** button at the bottom of the form:

    <img
      src="/images/f25.png"
      alt="F25 Pn"
      title="F25 Pn"
      className="mx-auto"
      style={{ width:"84%" }}
    />

    1. A **Select Page Range** popup appears.
    2. Choose the appropriate option to attach the document:
       - **Current Page** – Attach only the page currently visible.
       - **Page Range** – Specify a custom range using the **Start Page** and **End Page** fields.
       - **All Pages** – Attach the entire document.
       - **No Attachment** – Proceed without attaching any pages.
    3. Click **Attach & Save** to finalize your selection and attach the document to the record.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attachments">
    The **Attachments** section displays all documents linked to the current record. Here's how you can interact with attachments:

    ![F26 Pn](/images/f26.png)

    1. **Created At: **Shows the date and time the file was attached to the record.
    2. **File Name: **Displays the exact name of the uploaded or attached file.
    3. **Actions: **Use the icons in the **Actions** column to manage attachments:
       - **View**: Click the eye icon to preview the attachment in the viewer.
       - **Download**: Click the download icon to save a copy of the file to your device.
       - **Delete**: Click the trash icon to permanently remove the attachment from the record.

    <Note>
      Once deleted, attachments cannot be recovered. Ensure accuracy before removing files.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>
