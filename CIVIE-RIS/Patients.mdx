# Patient Management Overview

The Patient module in CIVIE serves as the central hub for managing patient records. It provides a comprehensive worklist where users can search, filter, edit, merge, and organize patient data efficiently. This module is essential for administrators, front-desk staff, and healthcare providers to manage patient demographics, contact details, medical records, and appointment history.

## Key Features

- **Patient Worklist** – Complete list of patients with detailed information
- **Search & Filters** – Quick patient record location tools
- **New Patient Registration** – Manual entry of new patient details
- **Record Management** – Modify demographics, contact details, and insurance
- **Record Merging** – Identify and merge duplicate patient records
- **Data Export** – Download patient data in .xls format

## Search and Filter Functions

### Using Search Features

<Steps>
  <Step title="Use Search Fields">
    - Utilize the search bar to find patients by:
      - MRN
      - Name
      - Date of Birth (DOB)
      - Phone Number
      - Email
    - Results update automatically in real-time
  </Step>

  <Step title="Apply Filters">
    1. Click on the **Filters** panel
    2. Select from available options:
       - Alerts
       - MRN
       - First, Last, or Middle Name
       - Date of Birth (DOB)
    3. Click **Apply** to update results
    4. Use **Reset Filters** to clear all filters
  </Step>

  <Step title="Use Worklist Options">
    - Select date range from **Worklist Range** dropdown
      - Today
      - Last Week
      - Custom Date Range
    - Use **Worklist Category** for specific criteria filtering
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing Patient Records

### Adding New Patients

<Steps>
  <Step title="Initiate Patient Registration">
    Click the **+ New Patient** button in the top-right corner
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete Required Information">
    Fill in the following sections:
    - Patient Demographics
    - Contact Information
    - Address Details
    - Emergency Contacts
    - Insurance Information
    - Employer Details (if applicable)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save Record">
    Click **Save** to create the new patient record
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Editing Patient Information

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Edit Mode">
    Click the **Edit** icon next to the patient record
  </Step>

  <Step title="Update Information">
    Modify necessary fields:
    - Demographics
    - Contact Details
    - Insurance Information
    - Other relevant data
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save Changes">
    Click **Save** to apply updates
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Merging Patient Records

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start Merge Process">
    Click the **Merge Patient** button at the top
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Records">
    - Check boxes appear next to patient records
    - Select two records to merge
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and Confirm">
    1. Click **Finish Selection**
    2. Choose information to retain
    3. Click **Next**
    4. Add merge comments if needed
    5. Click **Save** to complete
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Detailed Patient View

Access comprehensive patient information through the following tabs:

### Overview Tab
- Today's appointments
- Previous appointment history
- Filter options for appointments
- Add orders and notes

### Orders Tab
- Search and view patient orders
- Create new orders

### Insurance Tab
- View active/inactive insurance

- Verify eligibility

## Medical History Management

### Adding Medical Information

<Steps>
  <Step title="Record Allergies">
    - Click **+ Add Allergies**
    - Enter allergy details:
      - Name
      - Reaction
      - Severity
      - Onset Date
  </Step>

  <Step title="Document Medications">
    - Click **+ Add Medications**
    - Specify:
      - Medication Name
      - Dose
      - Unit
  </Step>

  <Step title="Log Medical Problems">
    - Click **+ Add Problems**
    - Include:
      - Diagnosis
      - Date
      - Status
      - Synopsis
  </Step>

  <Step title="Update Medical History">
    - Click **+ Past Medical History**
    - Enter:
      - Start/End Dates
      - Description
  </Step>

  <Step title="Record Surgical History">
    - Click **+ Past Surgical History**
    - Document:
      - Diagnosis
      - Dates
      - Description
  </Step>
</Steps>
